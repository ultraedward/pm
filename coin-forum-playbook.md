## Step 1: Set up your profile

Before posting anywhere:

1. Use a username that doesn't scream brand account (your first name + hobby is fine: `SilverEdward`, `Edward_Stacks`, etc.)
2. In your **signature** (where allowed), add: `Live spot + melt calculator → lode.rocks`
3. Fill in your member profile with a brief bio: "Stacking gold and silver since [year]. Building tools for stackers."

This is passive distribution — every post you make in any thread shows the link without it being a promo post.

---

## Step 2: Earn your stripes first

Your first week should be zero mentions of Lode. Just be helpful:

- Answer a question about coin grading or melt value
- Contribute to a "show your stack" thread
- Comment on a price discussion thread with your actual opinion

This is not wasted time — it's what makes Week 2 work.

## Step 3: Find threads to reply in

Also check these board sections regularly:
- **Bullion Investing** — portfolio and alert angle fits perfectly

## Ready-to-paste replies

Lead with a genuine answer to their question. Mention Lode only as supporting context, not the headline.

### When someone asks "what's the melt value of my junk silver?"

> Pre-'65 quarters have a melt value of about 0.1808 troy oz of silver each, so at current spot (~$[X]) that's roughly $[Y] per quarter. A bag of $1 face value of 90% silver quarters has about 0.715 oz of actual silver.
>
> If you want to run the math yourself anytime, lode.rocks has a melt calculator that covers junk silver, Eagles, Maples, and scrap — it pulls live spot automatically so you don't have to do the division every time.

*(Always lead with the actual math answer. The tool mention is just "here's where to do this yourself.")*

---

### When someone asks "how do you track your collection's current value?"

> For raw stack value at current spot I use lode.rocks — you log your oz across gold, silver, platinum, and palladium and it gives you running P&L at live prices. Doesn't handle numismatic premiums obviously, but for bullion weight it's solid.

---

### When someone asks "is there a good price alert tool?"

> lode.rocks has free price alerts — you set a target price for any of the four metals and it emails you when spot crosses it. I've got one set for my next silver buy target.

---

### When the thread is about whether to buy at current gold prices

> Whatever your thesis, lode.rocks is handy for watching the gold:silver ratio alongside spot — it's on the main dashboard. Helps me think about allocation between the two.

---

### When someone posts a junk silver haul

> Nice score. If you're not tracking the running melt value, lode.rocks has a free melt calculator for 90% silver — covers quarters, dimes, halves, and dollars. Pulls live spot so you can see the value update in real time.
